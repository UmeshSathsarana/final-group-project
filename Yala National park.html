Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka. Located in the southeast region of the island, it covers an area of 979 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of wildlife.

The park is renowned for its large population of leopards, making it one of the best places in the world to observe these elusive big cats. Other notable species found in Yala include elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species.

Yala is divided into five blocks, with Block 1 being the most frequently visited by tourists. The park features a mix of ecosystems, including dry forests, wetlands, and sandy beaches along the coastline.

Visitors to Yala can enjoy safari tours, which provide an opportunity to see the park's wildlife up close. The best time to visit is during the dry season, from February to July, when animals congregate around water sources.

Conservation efforts are essential to protect the park's biodiversity and ensure the survival of its wildlife. Responsible tourism practices and anti-poaching measures are crucial for preserving Yala's natural beauty for future generations.
