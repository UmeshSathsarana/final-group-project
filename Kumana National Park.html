Kumana National Park

Kumana National Park, located on the southeast coast of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its rich avian fauna. The park is a birdwatcher's paradise with a vast array of bird species, especially during the migratory season when the park becomes a temporary home for thousands of birds.

Covering an area of about 391 square kilometers, Kumana is characterized by its large swamps, lagoons, and mangroves, which create ideal conditions for birdlife. The park's Kumana Villu, a 200-hectare mangrove swamp, is particularly famous for being the nesting and breeding ground for many species of water birds.

In addition to its avian population, Kumana National Park is home to various other wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and a variety of reptiles and mammals. The park's diverse ecosystems also support a rich array of plant species, contributing to its biological diversity.

Visitors to Kumana can explore the park's natural beauty and wildlife through safari tours, which offer opportunities to see the wildlife in their natural habitats. The park's serene and relatively undisturbed environment provides a peaceful and immersive nature experience.

Whether you're an avid birdwatcher or a nature enthusiast, Kumana National Park offers a unique and memorable wildlife experience, showcasing the natural splendor of Sri Lanka's southeastern coast.
