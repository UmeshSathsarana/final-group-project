Sri Lankan Snakes

Venomous Snakes

Sri Lanka is home to a variety of venomous snakes, some of which are highly dangerous to humans. These snakes play a crucial role in the ecosystem by controlling rodent populations and maintaining the balance of nature. Here are some of the most notable venomous snakes found in Sri Lanka:

Russell's Viper

The Russell's viper is one of the most dangerous snakes in Sri Lanka. It is responsible for the majority of snakebite incidents in the country. This snake is highly venomous, and its bite can cause severe pain, swelling, and bleeding. Immediate medical attention is required if bitten by a Russell's viper.

Cobra

The cobra is another highly venomous snake found in Sri Lanka. Known for its distinctive hood, the cobra's venom can cause paralysis and respiratory failure if left untreated. Cobras are often found in rural areas and are revered in Sri Lankan culture.

Krait

Kraits are highly venomous snakes that are nocturnal and often enter human dwellings at night. Their venom is neurotoxic and can cause muscle paralysis. The common krait and the Sri Lankan krait are the two species found on the island.

Non-Venomous Snakes

Sri Lanka also hosts a variety of non-venomous snakes, which are harmless to humans and play a significant role in the ecosystem. These snakes help control pest populations and are an essential part of the food chain.

Common Rat Snake

The common rat snake is a large, non-venomous snake found throughout Sri Lanka. It is an excellent climber and often enters homes in search of rodents. Despite its size, it is harmless to humans and helps control rat populations.

Green Vine Snake

The green vine snake is a slender, bright green snake that blends perfectly with foliage. It is mildly venomous, but its venom is not harmful to humans. This snake preys on small animals and birds and is often found in gardens and forests.

Python

Pythons are large constrictor snakes that are found in Sri Lanka's forests and wetlands. They are non-venomous and kill their prey by constriction. Pythons are known to grow to impressive lengths and are an important part of the ecosystem.
