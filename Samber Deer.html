Sambar Deer

The sambar deer (Rusa unicolor) is a large deer native to the Indian subcontinent, South China, and Southeast Asia. These deer are known for their distinctive large antlers and rugged build, making them a significant presence in their habitats.

Sambar deer inhabit a variety of ecosystems, including forests, grasslands, and wetlands. They are primarily nocturnal and are known to forage for a wide range of plant material, including leaves, fruits, and bark.

In Sri Lanka, sambar deer are commonly found in national parks and protected areas such as Yala, Horton Plains, and Wilpattu. They play a crucial role in the ecosystem as prey for large predators like leopards and as herbivores that help maintain the balance of vegetation.

Conservation efforts are important for the sambar deer as they face threats from habitat loss and hunting. Protected areas and wildlife reserves are essential for their survival, providing safe habitats where they can thrive.
