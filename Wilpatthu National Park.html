Wilpattu National Park

Wilpattu National Park is located on the northwest coast of Sri Lanka and is one of the oldest and largest national parks in the country. The park spans over 1,317 square kilometers and is renowned for its numerous "Willus" (natural lakes), which are a unique feature of this park and a critical source of water for the wildlife.

Wilpattu is famous for its diverse range of habitats including dense scrub jungles, grasslands, and wetlands, making it a haven for a variety of wildlife. The park is home to leopards, elephants, sloth bears, and a multitude of bird species, making it an ideal destination for wildlife enthusiasts and bird watchers.

One of the highlights of visiting Wilpattu is the opportunity to spot the elusive leopard. The park is one of the best places in Sri Lanka to see these majestic cats in their natural habitat. Additionally, the park's dense forest and numerous water bodies provide a perfect environment for bird species, including several endemic and migratory birds.

Visitors to Wilpattu can also explore the historical and cultural sites within the park, including ancient ruins and temples, which add a fascinating historical dimension to the wildlife experience. The park's remote location and less crowded nature offer a tranquil and immersive experience with nature.

Whether you're seeking the thrill of spotting a leopard or enjoying the serene beauty of the natural lakes, Wilpattu National Park offers a unique and unforgettable wildlife experience.
