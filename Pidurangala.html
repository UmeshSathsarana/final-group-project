Pidurangala

Pidurangala is a massive rock formation located a few kilometers north of the historical Sigiriya in Sri Lanka. It is a popular destination for hikers and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area, including a stunning view of the Sigiriya rock fortress.

The climb to the summit of Pidurangala involves a moderate hike through a forested path and some rock scrambling near the top. Along the way, visitors can explore the ancient Pidurangala Temple, which dates back to the 5th century and features a large reclining Buddha statue.

Once at the top, hikers are rewarded with panoramic views of the lush landscape and the iconic silhouette of Sigiriya in the distance. The flat summit of Pidurangala is an excellent spot to watch the sunrise or sunset, providing a serene and awe-inspiring experience.

Pidurangala is not only a place of natural beauty but also a site of historical and cultural significance. It offers a quieter and less crowded alternative to the more famous Sigiriya, making it a perfect destination for those seeking tranquility and a connection with nature.
